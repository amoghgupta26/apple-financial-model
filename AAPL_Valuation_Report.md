## Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive bottom-up financial model and intrinsic value analysis for **Apple Inc. (AAPL)**. Using five years of historical data (FY2019–FY2023) and a five-year forward projection (FY2024E–FY2028E), we derive an implied base-case share price of approximately **$172**, representing ~12% downside from the reference price of $195.

While Apple remains a best-in-class franchise with unmatched cash generation and brand loyalty, the current valuation already prices in a significant portion of the growth optionality from Services expansion and AI integration. We rate the stock a **HOLD** and await a better entry point or a catalyst that materially changes the earnings trajectory.

## 1. Company Overview

Apple Inc. designs, develops, and sells consumer electronics, software, and digital services. Founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in 1976, Apple today is the world's most valuable company by market capitalization, with revenues of $383 billion in FY2023.

**Business Segments:**

| Segment | FY2023 Revenue | % of Total | Gross Margin |
|---------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| iPhone | $200.6B | 52.3% | ~35% |
| Mac | $29.4B | 7.7% | ~35% |

| Wearables & Accessories | $39.8B | 10.4% | ~30% |
| **Services** | **$85.2B** | **22.2%** | **~70%** |

The **Services** segment — including App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, Apple TV+, Apple Arcade, Apple Pay, and AppleCare — has become the crown jewel of Apple's business model. With ~70% gross margins versus ~35% for hardware, every incremental Services dollar is worth roughly twice as much as a hardware dollar to the bottom line.

## 3. Projection Assumptions & Rationale

### Revenue Growth

We project FY2024–2028 revenue growth of 4–8% annually, building toward a terminal growth rate of 3%.

**FY2024E (4.3%):** iPhone 15 cycle running its course; Services continues to compound; partial recovery in China.

**FY2025E–2026E (7–8%):** Apple Intelligence (generative AI) features drive meaningful iPhone upgrade cycle acceleration; India retail expansion contributes incremental revenue; Vision Pro ecosystem begins to scale.

**FY2027E–2028E (6.5–7.5%):** Revenue base becomes large, moderating percentage growth; ongoing Services compounding partially offsets hardware maturation.

### Margin Evolution

**Gross Margin** expands modestly from 44.1% to 45.5%, driven by:
- Services mix increasing from 22% → 28% of revenue
- Hardware supply chain optimization
- Partially offset by pricing competition in key markets

**EBITDA Margin** stays approximately flat at 32–33%, as increasing R&D investment in AI/spatial computing offsets gross margin expansion.

### Key Assumptions Check

A useful sanity check: Apple's FY2028E Revenue of ~$541B implies a Services revenue run-rate of ~$150B — roughly 1.8x the FY2023 level. This requires 12% annual Services growth, which aligns with recent trends (Services grew 16% in FY2023).

## 4. DCF Valuation — Detailed Build

### Unlevered Free Cash Flow Build

Unlevered FCF (UFCF) represents the cash the business generates for **all** capital providers (debt and equity), before considering the financing structure. It is the correct cash flow metric for a WACC-based DCF.

```
EBIT
 × (1 − Tax Rate)       → Tax-effected NOPAT
 + D&A                  → Non-cash add-back
 − CapEx                → Maintenance + growth investment
 − ΔWorking Capital     → Cash consumed by growth
= Unlevered FCF
```

| Year | Revenue | EBIT | NOPAT | + D&A | - CapEx | - ΔNWC | = UFCF |
|------|---------|------|-------|-------|---------|--------|--------|
| FY2024E | $400B | $121B | $102B | $12B | $12B | $1B | ~$101B |
| FY2025E | $428B | $131B | $111B | $12B | $12B | $1B | ~$110B |
| FY2026E | $462B | $143B | $121B | $13B | $13B | $2B | ~$119B |
| FY2027E | $497B | $155B | $131B | $13B | $13B | $2B | ~$129B |
| FY2028E | $529B | $165B | $139B | $14B | $14B | $2B | ~$137B |

### WACC Calculation

```
Cost of Equity (CAPM):
  Ke = Rf + β × ERP
  Ke = 4.25% + 1.25 × 5.50% = 11.1%

After-Tax Cost of Debt:
  Kd(AT) = 4.80% × (1 − 15.5%) = 4.06%

WACC:
  WACC = 11.1% × 92% + 4.06% × 8% = 10.0%
```

**Why β = 1.25?** Apple has historically had a beta of 1.1–1.3 versus the S&P 500. Given its size and index weight, it tends to move closely with the market but with slightly higher volatility — particularly around product launch cycles and macro-sensitive consumer demand.

### Terminal Value

We use two methods and average them:

**Gordon Growth Model:**
```
TV = UFCF_FY28 × (1 + g) / (WACC − g)
TV = $137B × (1.03) / (0.10 − 0.03)
TV = $2,017B (undiscounted)
PV of TV = $2,017B / (1.10)^5 = $1,253B
```

**Exit EV/EBITDA Multiple Method:**
```
TV = EBITDA_FY28 × Exit Multiple
TV = $174B × 22.0x = $3,828B (undiscounted)
PV of TV = $3,828B / (1.10)^5 = $2,377B
```

**Blended TV (average):** ~$1,815B (PV)

## 7. Risk Factors

### Upside Risks
- **Apple Intelligence adoption** drives iPhone upgrade cycle acceleration; ASP expansion
- **Services margins** exceed model assumptions as App Store and Apple Pay scale
- **India** becomes a $30–40B revenue market by FY2027 (currently ~$6B)
- **Interest rate cuts** reduce WACC, boosting terminal value significantly

### Downside Risks  
- **China headwinds:** Revenue exposure to China (~20% of sales) faces regulatory and geopolitical risks
- **App Store regulation:** EU Digital Markets Act and DOJ antitrust action could structurally reduce App Store take rates
- **Innovation plateau:** Without a true next platform (Vision Pro is expensive and niche), hardware growth may decelerate faster than modeled
- **Margin normalization:** Supply chain disruptions or component cost inflation could compress hardware margins

## 8. Investment Conclusion

**Rating: HOLD | Price Target: $172 (Base) | Bull: $230 | Bear: $130**

Apple is one of the highest-quality businesses ever built. The combination of unmatched brand equity, an installed base of 2 billion+ active devices, a high-margin and growing Services engine, and extraordinary capital return discipline makes it a perennial long-term compounder.

However, at $195/share, we believe the stock is **pricing in a relatively optimistic scenario**. Our base-case DCF implies ~12% downside, and even in a bull scenario with accelerated AI-driven upgrades and Services margin expansion, the upside to $230 represents only ~18% — a modest return for the risk taken.

**We would become more constructive at or below $160–165**, where the risk/reward improves meaningfully and the implied assumptions for WACC and terminal growth become more conservative.
